# Public / government bid

## What changes vs. the default pipeline

| Stage | Public-sector change |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Takeoff** | Unit-price schedules with **owner-estimated quantities** — take off to verify, but bid the schedule's quantities. Flag over/under-run risk on big items. Public specs are often prescriptive (no "or equal" without formal substitution request pre-bid). |
| **Procurement** | Sealed bid, public opening, **no post-bid negotiation**. Sub quotes must be locked pre-opening; DBE/SBE/MBE goals may dictate which subs you can use. ODP materials get carved out for owner POs. |
| **Scope** | Scope is fixed by the documents — clarifications go through pre-bid RFIs and addenda only. Qualified or conditioned bids are **non-responsive** (rejected). Exclusions lists that work on private work will get the bid thrown out. |
| **Estimate** | Add bid bond, 100% P&P bond, certified-payroll admin (if federal), escalation for long award cycles. Deduct sales tax on ODP-flagged materials only if the ODP deduct is structured per the bid form. Tighter OH&P — openings are public and competitive. |
| **Proposal** | The **bid form governs**: exact acknowledgment of every addendum, all blanks filled, bond attached, licenses/registrations current, DBE forms complete. One missed signature = non-responsive. |
| **Audit** | Check responsiveness items (addenda, bond %, forms) before checking math. Check SOV for front-loading (owners audit). Confirm unit-price extensions against the owner's quantities, not yours. |

## Sector red flags / deal-breakers

- [ ] Bonding capacity insufficient for this job + current backlog — **stop** before spending takeoff hours.
- [ ] Owner requires advance prequalification (FDOT Certificate of Qualification per FS 337.14 / Rule 14-22 for jobs > $250k; school-board or county annual prequal) and we are not currently prequalified or the window has closed — **stop** before takeoff.
- [ ] Mandatory pre-bid already occurred and we missed it — non-responsive; drop the pursuit.
- [ ] License mismatch: solicitation requires a license class/registration we don't hold (see DBPR classes in `florida-code.md` §5).
- [ ] Federal funds present but no WD in the docs — RFI immediately; a WD added by addendum late can blow up labor pricing.
- [ ] DBE goal higher than achievable sub coverage in this market and no good-faith-effort path.
- [ ] LDs disproportionate to job size, or schedule is unachievable as bid (no negotiation later).
- [ ] Unit-price quantities obviously wrong vs. takeoff and owner refuses to correct by addendum — over/under-run exposure.
- [ ] Owner's ODP procedure strips OH&P from deducted amounts (fee erosion).
- [ ] Bid validity period longer than our price protection from subs/suppliers — escalation gap.
- [ ] Protest-prone owner/low spread history — margin already thin; weigh pursuit cost.
- [ ] Local wage ordinance in docs during preemption transition — price it, and flag for counsel (HB 433; verify enforceability).

# New residential construction

## What changes vs. the default pipeline

| Stage | What residential-new changes |
|-------|------------------------------|
| **Takeoff** | Quantify by **conditioned SF vs. under-roof SF** (garage, lanai, porches priced separately). Heavy 03/04/06 structure; finishes measured room-by-room by **finish tier**. Count every selection item (plumbing fixtures, lighting, appliances, tile fields) — each becomes an allowance line. |
| **Procurement** | Few formal specs; product approvals (FL# / NOA) substitute for spec sections on the envelope. Long-lead: impact windows/doors (8–16+ wks), garage doors, cabinetry, appliances. Truss shop drawings and engineering are a critical-path submittal. |
| **Scope** | Classify every owner touchpoint: **owner-supplied appliances**, pool by separate licensed pool contractor (Div 13), well/septic vs. municipal, low-voltage/smart-home. Termite treatment + certificate, blower door + duct test, elevation certificates all get explicit lines. |
| **Estimate** | Every allowance = **a line + a dollar cap + a reconciliation rule**. Sales tax on materials (contractor is consumer — see base doc). GCs run lean (working super, no trailer) but schedule-driven interest/carry matters on spec homes. |
| **Proposal** | Add: allowance schedule, draw schedule matching the lender's, deposit/escrow handling (FL 501.1375), warranty statement, selections deadline calendar, FL 558 / Ch. 713 statutory notices in the contract. |
| **Audit** | Check $/SF against tier band; check allowance total as % of contract (creep risk); check energy-test, termite, elevation-certificate, impact-glazing lines exist; check no double-markup on allowance items. |

---

## Sector red flags / deal-breakers

- [ ] Code path unresolved — 3-story/grade-plane/egress ambiguity (FBC-R vs. FBC-B) not settled with AHJ.
- [ ] Flood zone VE or AE with no geotech and no surveyed BFE/DFE — foundation cost is a guess.
- [ ] Buyer wants fixed price with **unselected finishes and no allowance caps** — allowance creep guaranteed.
- [ ] Deposit handling that violates FL 501.1375 escrow/waiver rules, or owner demanding large deposits with no written waiver.
- [ ] Lender draw schedule mismatched to cost curve (e.g., 10% at slab on a pile foundation job) — negative cash-flow contract.
- [ ] No ARB approval in hand where an HOA governs — schedule and material selections not real yet.
- [ ] Well/septic assumed but no soil/perc data, or septic setbacks don't fit the lot with the pool.
- [ ] Owner-supplied materials beyond appliances (tile, fixtures "buyer will provide") — warranty/schedule trap; qualify hard or decline.
- [ ] Teardown/infill lot with unpermitted existing structures or unknown utilities — demo, tree-mitigation, and impact-fee credits unverified.
- [ ] Contract silent on FL 558, warranty scope, and selections deadlines.

# New commercial construction

## What changes vs. the default pipeline

| Stage | Commercial-new deltas |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Takeoff** | Full site/civil takeoff (Div 31/32/33) is real money, not an afterthought. Quantify utility extensions to point of connection, retention/detention ponds, and paving/striping incl. **accessible stalls at 12 ft + 5 ft aisle (FL 553.5041)**. Separate shell vs. tenant-improvement quantities from day one. |
| **Procurement** | Delegated-design trades (trusses, precast/tilt, curtain wall, Div 21, Div 28) need **design-build subs with engineer-sealed submittals** — prequalify for engineering capacity, not just install price. Long-lead: switchgear, RTUs, elevators, storefront/curtain wall with FL#/NOA. |
| **Scope** | Draw the **core & shell / warm shell / turnkey** line explicitly (see §7). Assign fire alarm (28) vs. sprinkler (21) vs. fire pump/FDC interfaces. Assign SWPPP maintenance, threshold-inspector cost, special-inspections cost, impact fees — each to exactly one party. |
| **Estimate** | Div 01 built from schedule and staffing, **never a bare %** — 12–24 month durations make GCs the swing item. Carry escalation on long-lead packages. Price delegated-design engineering inside each sub's number. |
| **Proposal** | Qualifications must state: who pays impact/mobility fees, threshold inspection, special inspections, builder's risk; TI exclusions; utility connection/tap fees; permit fees by AHJ valuation. SOV per AIA G702/G703. |
| **Audit** | Check $/SF against sector bands (§9), fire protection priced (21 AND 28), threshold inspection carried if triggered, sales tax on materials, no double-markup of design-build subs, every division priced or excluded. |

---

## Sector red flags / deal-breakers

- [ ] No geotech report on a tilt-wall or heavy-slab job (pad certification, dewatering, deep foundations unknown).
- [ ] Threshold-trigger geometry (>3 stories / >50 ft / big assembly) with no threshold inspection carried by anyone.
- [ ] Bid docs silent on **impact/mobility fees** or utility connection fees — six-figure hole.
- [ ] ERP / SDP / plat not yet approved but schedule assumes immediate NTP.
- [ ] 2-story building with no elevator and no FL 553.509 waiver in hand.
- [ ] No fire-flow test data and no fire pump in the documents.
- [ ] "Shell" undefined — no tenant/landlord scope matrix; TI boundary ambiguous.
- [ ] OCIP/CCIP mentioned but sub quotes not de-scoped for insurance.
- [ ] Delegated-design packages with no design-build sub identified (fire alarm is the usual orphan).
- [ ] LDs with no schedule float and permit timeline outside GC control.
- [ ] Federal funding in the stack with no Davis-Bacon wage rates in the labor pricing.
- [ ] Pay-when-paid + long owner draw cycle on a thin-margin lump sum.
- [ ] Schedule spans hurricane season(s) with no weather-day definition in supplementary conditions and no storm-prep money in Div 01.

# Tenant improvement / buildout

## What changes vs. the default pipeline

| Stage | TI-specific change |
|-------|--------------------|
| **Takeoff** | Quantities come from a **field-verification walk**, not just drawings — as-builts lie. Add above-ceiling survey, panel schedules, RTU nameplates, demising wall ratings. Demo takeoff is as important as new-work takeoff. |
| **Procurement** | **Landlord-mandated vendors** (fire alarm, sprinkler, roofing, BAS — see §5) cannot be competitively bid — carry their quotes or a fat allowance. Pre-purchase long-lead items against an 8–14 week schedule. |
| **Scope** | Add lease-driven scope: work letter, building standards, restoration clauses, landlord approval cycles. Classify every demo item stay/go/salvage. Assign tie-in scope (drain-down, fire watch, shutdown coordination) to a named party. |
| **Estimate** | Contingency **10–15%** (existing conditions), not the new-construction norm. Div 01 carries after-hours premiums, protection, escorts. Price mandated-vendor quotes as-is, no shopping. |
| **Proposal** | Qualifications must state field-verification limits, hazmat exclusions/allowances, landlord fees carried or excluded, after-hours assumptions, and what the TI allowance does/doesn't cover. |
| **Audit** | Check: hazmat survey addressed, path-of-travel (ADA) carried, mandated vendors priced, fire-watch/impairment costs present, contingency ≥10%, restoration clause addressed, $/SF within the TI band for the use type. |

## Sector red flags / deal-breakers

- [ ] No asbestos survey provided on a pre-1981 building — **stop; survey before demo bid** (FL Ch. 469 / NESHAP).
- [ ] Bid due before a verification walk is possible — qualify heavily or decline.
- [ ] Panel schedules missing/full and drawings add load — service upgrade lurking.
- [ ] Change of occupancy (office→assembly/medical) not addressed on code summary.
- [ ] Ground-floor TI in AE/VE flood zone approaching the 50% substantial-improvement threshold.
- [ ] Mandated-vendor list not provided — you're bidding trades you can't buy.
- [ ] Work letter/lease exhibits not in the bid package — scope boundary unknown.
- [ ] Restaurant TI with no grease interceptor shown — exterior site work hiding in a "TI."
- [ ] Restoration clause requires removal of what you're building — advise client.
- [ ] Schedule assumes permit + LL approval overlap that the LL doesn't allow.
- [ ] R-22 or 15+ yr RTUs shown "reuse existing" with no condition report.
- [ ] Occupied healthcare adjacency with no ICRA scope or budget.
- [ ] Recorded FS 713.10 lien prohibition + thin-credit tenant + allowance paid in arrears —
  your lien is leasehold-only; secure payment another way or decline.
- [ ] Older building / multiple prior tenants with no as-built history — unpermitted legacy
  work discovered at demo becomes your permit problem; qualify that correction of
  pre-existing code violations and unpermitted prior work is excluded / handled by change order.
