# New Residential Construction (Florida)

Sector reference for **new single-family, duplex, and townhouse construction** —
custom, semi-custom, and spec/production homes. Builds on
[florida-code.md](florida-code.md), [estimating-methodology.md](estimating-methodology.md),
and [csi-divisions.md](csi-divisions.md); this file covers only what residential-new
**changes or adds**. The two forces that dominate this sector are the **code path**
(FBC-Residential vs. FBC-Building) and the **money path** (lender draws, buyer deposits,
selections/allowances) — get both right before pricing anything.

---

## 1. What changes vs. the default pipeline

| Stage | What residential-new changes |
|-------|------------------------------|
| **Takeoff** | Quantify by **conditioned SF vs. under-roof SF** (garage, lanai, porches priced separately). Heavy 03/04/06 structure; finishes measured room-by-room by **finish tier**. Count every selection item (plumbing fixtures, lighting, appliances, tile fields) — each becomes an allowance line. |
| **Procurement** | Few formal specs; product approvals (FL# / NOA) substitute for spec sections on the envelope. Long-lead: impact windows/doors (8–16+ wks), garage doors, cabinetry, appliances. Truss shop drawings and engineering are a critical-path submittal. |
| **Scope** | Classify every owner touchpoint: **owner-supplied appliances**, pool by separate licensed pool contractor (Div 13), well/septic vs. municipal, low-voltage/smart-home. Termite treatment + certificate, blower door + duct test, elevation certificates all get explicit lines. |
| **Estimate** | Every allowance = **a line + a dollar cap + a reconciliation rule**. Sales tax on materials (contractor is consumer — see base doc). GCs run lean (working super, no trailer) but schedule-driven interest/carry matters on spec homes. |
| **Proposal** | Add: allowance schedule, draw schedule matching the lender's, deposit/escrow handling (FL 501.1375), warranty statement, selections deadline calendar, FL 558 / Ch. 713 statutory notices in the contract. |
| **Audit** | Check $/SF against tier band; check allowance total as % of contract (creep risk); check energy-test, termite, elevation-certificate, impact-glazing lines exist; check no double-markup on allowance items. |

## 2. Code path — FBC-Residential vs. FBC-Building

- **FBC-Residential applies to:** detached **one- and two-family dwellings** and
  **townhouses ≤ 3 stories above grade plane** with a **separate means of egress**,
  plus their accessory structures (≤ 3 stories). (FBC-R Chapter 1 scope.)
- **Everything else is FBC-Building:** 4+ stories, stacked units (flats/condos —
  no separate egress per unit), live/work or mixed occupancy, 3+ unit buildings.
  Falling into FBC-B brings construction type, sprinklers (R-2), accessibility
  (FBC-Accessibility), and threshold-inspection questions — a different estimate.
- **Elevator edge case:** a private-residence elevator does **not** push the house
  into FBC-Building. Private-residence elevators are **exempt from FL Ch. 399**
  (state elevator registration/inspection) and are instead permitted/enforced by
  the local building department under the FBC, per ASME A17.1 residential provisions
  and Florida's residential-elevator safety amendments (Max Grablin Act door-clearance
  requirements — carry the compliant gate/door package, not the cheapest cab).
  What *does* flip the code path is **story count / grade plane / egress** — watch
  3-story-over-parking coastal homes where "stories above grade plane" is arguable;
  resolve with the AHJ before bid.
- **Townhouse/duplex separation:** FBC-R R302.2 common wall (2-hr, or two 1-hr
  walls) continuous foundation-to-roof deck with **no plumbing/mechanical
  penetrations** — forces MEP routing and adds cost in 03/04/06/07/09 per unit;
  duplex separation per R302.3. Confirm per-unit permits, NOCs, addresses, and
  utility meters — fees multiply per unit.
- Licensing: **CRC** (Residential) may build 1–3 family residences up to **two
  habitable stories over one uninhabitable story** (FL 489.105(3)(c)) — a
  3-habitable-story townhouse or 3-story-over-parking home requires a **CBC or CGC**.
  Pool, roofing, electrical, mechanical, plumbing remain specialty-licensed subs.

## 3. Energy code (FBC-Energy Conservation, Residential)

- Compliance paths: **prescriptive (R402/R403)** or **R405 simulated performance**
  ("Form R405" energy calc). Nearly all FL production/custom homes use **R405** —
  it buys trade-off flexibility (glass area vs. SEER2 vs. insulation). The energy
  rater/calc is a real line item; the calc must match the windows actually bought.
- **Blower door test required:** max **7 ACH50** in Climate Zones 1–2 (all of
  Florida), tested at 50 Pa by a qualified tester (FL 553.993 raters or licensed
  contractors per FL 489.105); written report to the building official. If the
  house tests **< 3 ACH50**, whole-house **mechanical ventilation** is required —
  a commonly missed HVAC add on tight custom homes.
- **Duct leakage test** (rough-in and/or postconstruction per R403.3, tested at
  25 Pa per ANSI/RESNET/ICC 380) unless ducts/air handler are entirely inside
  conditioned space. Carry the test **and** the sealing labor to pass it.

## 4. Wind / flood / termite / inspections — sector notes

Wind, HVHZ, WBDR, and flood-zone fundamentals: **see florida-code.md §§2–4.** Adds:

- **Impact glazing vs. shutters** is the single biggest envelope cost fork on a FL
  house; confirm which is specified, and that garage doors and entry doors are also
  rated. HVHZ (Miami-Dade/Broward) = NOA products only.
- **Elevation certificates (FEMA form, NFIP):** in SFHA (A/AE/VE zones) most
  communities and all construction lenders require an **under-construction EC**
  (after slab/lowest floor set) and a **finished-construction EC** (before CO).
  Two surveyor mobilizations — line them (usually GC-carried; confirm). Getting the
  slab an inch low is a tear-out; verify form-board survey before pour.
- **NFIP implications:** lender flood insurance on the buyer is priced off the
  finished EC; freeboard above BFE cuts the premium — owners may direct DFE higher
  than code minimum. Mechanical/electrical below DFE is a red-flag audit item.
- **Termite:** soil treatment or approved alternative required (FBC-R R318);
  the pest-control company issues **Certificates of Protective Treatment** to a
  jobsite posting board as treatments occur and a final **Certificate of Compliance**
  to the building department (FBC 104.2.6). Bait systems require a **5-year
  installation/monitoring contract delivered before slab pour**. Line the treatment
  AND the certificate/contract cost (Div 31).
- **Private provider inspections (FL 553.791):** commonly used by production
  builders to beat AHJ inspection queues. It is a GC cost (plan review + inspections)
  bought back partly through schedule; price it when the proforma is carry-sensitive.
- **Notice of Commencement** (FL Ch. 713) recorded before work; a copy must be filed
  with the permitting authority **before the first inspection when the direct
  contract exceeds $5,000** (FL 713.135). Lender usually insists it records *after*
  the mortgage — sequence matters.

## 5. Defect law, warranties, deposits (contract-shaping, not takeoff)

- **FL Ch. 558 presuit process:** owner must serve written notice of defect
  **≥ 60 days before suit** (≥ 120 days for associations > 20 parcels); contractor
  gets inspection/repair-offer rights. Keep 558 language in the contract; keep
  subs' warranty obligations flowing down.
- **Statute of repose: 7 years** (FL 95.11(3)(c), shortened from 10 by **SB 360,
  2023**), running from the **earliest** of TCO, CO, certificate of completion, or
  abandonment. Statute of limitations: **4 years**, running from the same
  earliest-trigger events (SB 360 changed this too); latent defects run from
  discovery or when they should have been discovered, but never beyond the 7-year
  repose. Retain project records ≥ 7 years past CO.
- **Implied warranties** of fitness, merchantability, and habitability attach to
  new-home sales under Florida common law (*Gable v. Silver* line of cases);
  **FL 553.835** bars implied-warranty claims for **offsite improvements**
  (roads, drainage, utilities not immediately supporting the home). Express written
  warranty (commonly 1yr workmanship / 2yr systems / structural via third-party
  program such as 2-10 or StrucSure) should define and supersede where permitted —
  verify wording with counsel.
- **Buyer deposits (FL 501.1375):** on 1–2 family new-home purchase contracts the
  buyer has the right (waivable **in writing**) to have deposits **up to 10% of the
  purchase price** held in escrow; builder may use escrowed funds for construction
  only after notice + posting a surety bond. Related permit-timing rules on deposits
  > 10% under FL 489.126 (verify with counsel) — build deposit handling into the
  contract, not the estimate.

## 6. Construction-loan reality

- **Lender draw schedules:** typically 5–8 draws tied to milestones (slab, lintel/
  tie-beam, dry-in, mechanical rough, drywall, final), each released only after a
  **lender inspection** (plus title update / lien-release package). Cash-flow gaps
  between spend and draw are the builder's — price carry, and match the **contract
  SOV to the lender's draw form** so nothing is stranded.
- Per-draw admin: inspection fees, conditional/unconditional releases from every
  sub/supplier who served a Notice to Owner (FL Ch. 713 — see base doc).
- **Builder's risk** carried by builder until CO (typically ~0.5–1.5% of hard cost
  range — quote it; coastal wind deductibles are large). Confirm who insures
  owner-supplied appliances/materials on site.
- **Allowance creep** is the sector's classic margin-killer: buyer selections
  exceed allowances (tile, lighting, plumbing trim, appliances), change orders lag,
  and the lender won't fund overages. Rules: every allowance has **(a)** a line,
  **(b)** a written cap incl. tax/freight/install basis (material-only vs. installed
  — say which), **(c)** a reconciliation rule (overage = signed CO paid **outside
  the loan, before order**). Selections deadline calendar in the contract.

## 7. HOA / fees / utilities

- **HOA & deed restrictions / ARB:** architectural review approval is often a
  precondition to permit; drives roof material, colors, fencing, mailbox, sod
  species, construction-hours, deposit/road-damage bonds, and builder registration
  fees. Get the ARB package cost and timeline into GCs (Div 01) — it can add
  4–8 weeks up front.
- **Impact fees** (transportation, schools, parks, fire) are **local ordinance**
  under Florida's Impact Fee Act (FL 163.31801 — verify current schedule with AHJ);
  on an SFR they can run four to five figures. Plus **water/sewer connection & tap
  fees** or, outside utility service: **well** (Div 33, WMD permit) + **septic/OSTDS**
  (DEP/DOH permit; engineered/mounded systems on bad soil are $$$ — get soil/perc
  data). State **who pays** fees (owner-direct is cleanest) — never silently absorb.
- **Tree ordinance:** removal permits, mitigation (replacement plantings or
  fee-in-lieu) for protected/specimen trees, and protection barricades during
  construction — get the tree survey and permit status before pricing sitework;
  FS 163.045 residential exemption needs arborist documentation, don't assume it
  applies.

| 02 | Small/absent | Lot clearing sometimes here or 31; demo of existing structure on teardowns |
| 03 | **Heavy** | Stem wall vs. monolithic slab (flood zone decides), post-tension vs. rebar, tie-beam/lintel fill, pool shell by pool sub — exclude |

| 10 | Small | Bath accessories, shelving, mailboxes (HOA spec) |

## 9. Markup & commercial posture

| Item | Residential-new norm (conventions — confirm per job) |
|------|------------------------------------------------------|
| **Payment & performance bond** | Rare on private homes; some lenders/HOAs require completion bond — price ~1–3% only if demanded (FL 255.05 is public work only) |
| **Retainage** | Often none owner→GC; builders commonly hold 5–10% on subs until punch — no statutory cap on private residential (public caps don't apply) |
| **General conditions** | ~5–10% of direct cost (lean); duration-driven — spec-home carry cost is the real Div 01 |
| **OH&P — custom (cost-plus)** | Fee commonly **12–20%** on cost; open-book; allowances reconciled at cost + fee |
| **OH&P — custom (fixed-price)** | **15–25%** combined OH&P; buyer selections risk priced in |
| **OH&P — spec/production** | Target **gross margin 18–25%** on sale price (market risk, not just build risk); interest carry eats it if schedule slips |
| **Contingency / escalation** | Estimating contingency ~3–5% on fixed-price custom (less on cost-plus, where allowances absorb it); material escalation on long-lead selections (windows, cabinets, appliances) priced to the order date, not bid date — per estimating-methodology.md §5; state whether it sits inside or outside OH&P |
| **Insurance** | GL + builder's risk (builder until CO); confirm wind/named-storm deductible; WC exemptions on small crews are an audit flag on subs |
| **Tax** | Contractor pays FL sales tax on materials (real-property contract — see base doc); allowance caps must state tax-in or tax-out |
| **Payment terms** | Deposit per FL 501.1375 escrow rules, then lender draws (5–8 milestones) or owner-funded schedule mirroring one; final at CO + punch |

**$/SF reasonableness bands** (vertical construction cost, conditioned SF, excl. land,
site development, impact fees, pool; FL market — **assumptions to confirm with current
local data**, per estimating-methodology.md §7):

| Tier | $/SF band | Markers |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Modest / production | ~$140–200 | Shingle roof, builder-grade package, slab-on-grade, vinyl windows w/ shutters |
| Mid / semi-custom | ~$200–300 | Impact glass, tile roofs appear, quartz counters, 9–10' plates |
| Custom | ~$300–450 | Full impact, tile/metal roof, custom millwork, smart-home, outdoor living |
| Luxury coastal | ~$450–800+ | HVHZ/VE-zone structure, pilings/stem walls, elevator, whole-house generator, high-end everything |

## 10. Sector red flags / deal-breakers

- [ ] Code path unresolved — 3-story/grade-plane/egress ambiguity (FBC-R vs. FBC-B) not settled with AHJ.
- [ ] Flood zone VE or AE with no geotech and no surveyed BFE/DFE — foundation cost is a guess.
- [ ] Buyer wants fixed price with **unselected finishes and no allowance caps** — allowance creep guaranteed.
- [ ] Deposit handling that violates FL 501.1375 escrow/waiver rules, or owner demanding large deposits with no written waiver.
- [ ] Lender draw schedule mismatched to cost curve (e.g., 10% at slab on a pile foundation job) — negative cash-flow contract.
- [ ] No ARB approval in hand where an HOA governs — schedule and material selections not real yet.
- [ ] Well/septic assumed but no soil/perc data, or septic setbacks don't fit the lot with the pool.
- [ ] Owner-supplied materials beyond appliances (tile, fixtures "buyer will provide") — warranty/schedule trap; qualify hard or decline.
- [ ] Teardown/infill lot with unpermitted existing structures or unknown utilities — demo, tree-mitigation, and impact-fee credits unverified.
- [ ] Contract silent on FL 558, warranty scope, and selections deadlines.

## 11. Deliverable additions (beyond the standard bid package)

- **Allowance schedule** — every allowance: line, cap, basis (material-only vs. installed, tax-in/out), reconciliation rule, selection deadline.
- **Draw schedule / SOV** in the lender's format, mapped to milestones.
- **Specifications/finish schedule** by room and tier (substitutes for spec book).
- **Warranty statement** (1/2/structural or third-party program) + FL 558 contract language.
- **Energy compliance package** — Form R405 calc, blower-door/duct-test line, tester named.
- **Permit/fee matrix** — permit, impact, tap/connection, ARB fees, who pays each.
- **Exclusions list** naming: pool (by others), appliances (owner-supplied, connections by GC), window treatments, landscaping beyond code/ARB minimum, low-voltage devices beyond rough-in allowance.
- **Elevation-certificate plan** (SFHA jobs): form-board survey, under-construction EC, finished EC — who orders, who pays.

## 12. Open items to confirm per job

1. AHJ + FBC edition; FBC-R vs. FBC-B determination (stories above grade plane, egress).
2. Wind: V_ult, exposure, WBDR/HVHZ status → impact glazing or shutters?
3. Flood zone, BFE, community freeboard → DFE, foundation type, EC count.
4. Energy path (R405 vs. prescriptive) and who provides the calc/rating.
5. Termite method (soil treatment vs. bait — bait needs the 5-yr contract pre-slab).
6. Financing: lender named? Draw schedule, inspection regime, deposit structure & escrow/waiver per FL 501.1375.
7. HOA/ARB: approval status, builder deposits, construction rules, mandated finishes.
8. Utilities: municipal tap fees vs. well/septic (soil data, WMD/DOH permits).
9. Impact-fee schedule from AHJ; who pays which fees.
10. Selections status: what's picked, what's allowanced, deadlines calendar.
11. Owner-supplied items (appliances at minimum) and who insures/receives/sets them.
12. Pool/outdoor package: in contract as sub, separate contract, or excluded?
13. Private provider inspections (FL 553.791) — worth the cost for this schedule?
14. Warranty program (self vs. third-party) and contract language reviewed by counsel (558, repose, implied-warranty wording).
15. Geotech/soil data for pad fill, dewatering, deep foundations (coastal/muck lots).
16. Gas: natural gas available at lot? If LP: tank size/ownership, location (setbacks), who contracts the supplier.
17. Stormwater: NPDES applicability, county lot-grading plan and pre-CO drainage cert requirement.
