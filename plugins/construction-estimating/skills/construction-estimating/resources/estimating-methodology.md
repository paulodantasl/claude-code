# Estimating Methodology

How the estimator and auditor agents price work and sanity-check numbers. Pricing
varies by market, time, and project — these are **conventions and reasonableness
ranges, not quoted prices.** Always treat costs as assumptions to confirm with current
quotes, and label them as such.

## 1. Units of measure (standardize the takeoff)

| Unit | Used for |
|------|----------|
| CY | Concrete, earthwork (cubic yards) |
| SF / SY | Areas (square feet / square yards — paving, flooring) |
| LF | Linear (walls, curb, pipe, conduit, trim) |
| EA | Counts (fixtures, doors, equipment) |
| SQ | Roofing (1 SQ = 100 SF) |
| TON / LBS | Steel, rebar, asphalt, HVAC tonnage |
| MBF | Lumber (1,000 board feet) |
| LS | Lump sum |
| ALLOW | Allowance |

Cross-check quantities for reasonableness (e.g., rebar lbs per CY of concrete; CMU per
SF of wall; ductwork lbs per CFM). Flag outliers.

## 2. Cost components per line item

Each line carries up to four cost types: **M** material, **L** labor, **E** equipment,
**S** subcontract. Self-performed work is built up from M+L+E; subbed work is a single
**S** number that already includes the sub's own M/L/E/OH&P.

**Labor build-up:**
`labor $/unit = (crew hourly cost) ÷ (production rate in units/hr)`, or
`= (man-hours/unit) × (labor rate incl. burden)`.

- **Labor burden** (on top of base wage): FICA/Medicare ~7.65%, FUTA/SUTA, **workers'
  comp** (varies wildly by trade — low for finish trades, high for roofing/steel/concrete),
  GL, fringe/benefits. Composite burden commonly **25–45%**. Confirm WC rates by trade.
- Apply **productivity adjustments** for height, congestion, phasing, overtime,
  weather, and learning curve.

**Material:** unit material cost × quantity × (1 + **waste factor**). Add freight and
**Florida sales tax** (6% + county surtax) on materials for real-property contracts.

**Equipment:** owned (internal rate) or rented (hourly/daily/weekly/monthly). Include
**mobilization/demobilization**, fuel, operator (if not in labor), and small tools
(often a % of labor).

## 3. Typical waste / shrinkage factors

| Material | Waste |
|----------|-------|
| Ready-mix concrete | 5–10% |
| Rebar | ~5% |
| CMU / mortar | 3–5% |
| Dimensional lumber | ~10% |
| Gypsum board | 10–15% |
| Tile | ~10% (more on diagonals) |
| Roofing | 10–15% |
| Paint | ~5–10% |
| Earthwork fill | swell/shrink per geotech (often 10–25%) |

## 4. General Conditions & General Requirements (Div 01)

Time-driven project costs — scale with **schedule duration**:
- Project staff: PM, superintendent, PE, safety, admin (man-months × rate).
- Field office/trailer, temp toilets, temp power & water, internet.
- Dumpsters & disposal, daily/final cleaning, progress cleaning.
- Small tools/consumables, layout/survey, as-builts.
- Safety program/PPE, fall protection, fencing/barricades.
- Temporary protection, winterization (rare FL), weather.

GCs commonly run **8–15% of direct cost** depending on size/duration; verify against the
actual schedule and staffing, **don't just apply a percentage blindly.**

## 5. Markups (apply in the right order)

Typical build-up from direct cost to bid:
1. **Direct cost** (M+L+E+S by division)
2. **+ General Conditions** (Div 01)
3. **+ Escalation / contingency** (design or estimating contingency; material escalation)
4. **+ Insurance** — GL, builder's risk (often ~0.5–1.5% of cost; wrap-ups OCIP/CCIP differ)
5. **+ Bonds** — payment & performance, **~1–3% of contract** (sliding scale; verify if required — FL 255.05 on public work)
6. **+ Permit fees** — % of valuation, set by AHJ
7. **+ GC Overhead & Profit (OH&P)** — combined commonly **5–15%** (lower on large jobs,
   higher on small); subs already include their own OH&P in their S numbers.

Be explicit about whether bonds/insurance/permit are inside or outside OH&P, and whether
sales tax is already inside material costs. **Never double-mark-up subcontractor numbers.**

## 6. Bid-document items to always address

- **Allowances** — carry the specified dollar amount; note exclusions beyond it.
- **Alternates** — price add/deduct separately; keep base bid clean.
- **Unit prices** — provide for over/under-run items the owner requested.
- **Schedule of Values (SOV)** — division/phase breakdown for billing.
- **Qualifications / clarifications / exclusions** — must match the scope narrative.
- **Addenda** — incorporate ALL; list them on the proposal.

## 7. Reasonableness / sanity checks (for the auditor)

- **Extensions:** qty × unit = extended; subtotals foot; rounding consistent.
- **$/SF of building** lands in a plausible band for the building type and market.
- **Trade % of total** is plausible (e.g., on commercial: structure, MEP ~25–40% combined,
  finishes, etc. — flag a division that is implausibly large or tiny/missing).
- **Every CSI division** is either priced or explicitly excluded — no silent gaps.
- **Quantity ratios** (rebar/CY, CMU/SF, duct lbs/CFM, fixtures/SF) within norms.
- **Florida items present:** wind/impact products, flood provisions, termite treatment,
  energy testing, threshold inspection (if applicable), sales tax on materials, bonds.
- **Markups applied once, in order; no double-markup; subs not re-burdened.**
- **Allowances, alternates, unit prices, addenda** all addressed.
