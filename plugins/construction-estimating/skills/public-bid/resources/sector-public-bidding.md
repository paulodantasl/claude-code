# Public / Government Bidding (Florida)

Sector reference for hard-bid public work in Florida — state agencies, counties,
municipalities, school boards, and special districts. Public bids are won or lost on
**responsiveness and price**, not relationships: the forms, bonds, and acknowledgments
matter as much as the number. This doc covers what public work changes vs. the default
pipeline in `estimating-methodology.md`, `florida-code.md`, and `csi-divisions.md`.

---

## 1. What changes vs. the default pipeline

| Stage | Public-sector change |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Takeoff** | Unit-price schedules with **owner-estimated quantities** — take off to verify, but bid the schedule's quantities. Flag over/under-run risk on big items. Public specs are often prescriptive (no "or equal" without formal substitution request pre-bid). |
| **Procurement** | Sealed bid, public opening, **no post-bid negotiation**. Sub quotes must be locked pre-opening; DBE/SBE/MBE goals may dictate which subs you can use. ODP materials get carved out for owner POs. |
| **Scope** | Scope is fixed by the documents — clarifications go through pre-bid RFIs and addenda only. Qualified or conditioned bids are **non-responsive** (rejected). Exclusions lists that work on private work will get the bid thrown out. |
| **Estimate** | Add bid bond, 100% P&P bond, certified-payroll admin (if federal), escalation for long award cycles. Deduct sales tax on ODP-flagged materials only if the ODP deduct is structured per the bid form. Tighter OH&P — openings are public and competitive. |
| **Proposal** | The **bid form governs**: exact acknowledgment of every addendum, all blanks filled, bond attached, licenses/registrations current, DBE forms complete. One missed signature = non-responsive. |
| **Audit** | Check responsiveness items (addenda, bond %, forms) before checking math. Check SOV for front-loading (owners audit). Confirm unit-price extensions against the owner's quantities, not yours. |

## 2. FL public procurement landscape (who bids under what law)

| Authority | Governs | Key triggers |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **FL Ch. 255** | Public property & public works (all levels) | Bonds (255.05), local bid thresholds (255.20) |
| **FL 255.20** | County/municipal/special-district construction | Must **competitively award** projects > **$300,000** (construction) or > **$75,000** (electrical); base thresholds set in 2009 and adjusted by the ENR Building Cost Index change since January 1, 2009 (current effective triggers are well above the base figures) — confirm current adjusted figures with the AHJ |
| **FL Ch. 287** | State agency procurement of commodities/services | Category thresholds set by DMS; state construction runs through DMS rules |
| **FL 287.055 (CCNA)** | A/E, landscape architect, surveyor selection | **Qualifications-based**, price negotiated after ranking — relevant when pursuing design-build (designer selection) or CM work |
| **Local purchasing ordinances** | Each county/city/school board | Own thresholds, local-preference rules, protest procedures — **always pull the ordinance**; it supplements (never replaces) state law |

- **Delivery methods:** hard bid (ITB, low bid), RFP (best value), CM-at-Risk, design-build
  — this doc assumes hard bid; RFP/CM change the posture (fee-based, negotiated GMP).
- Local preference programs exist but vary; some are restricted on state-funded work
  (verify with counsel/AHJ per solicitation).

## 3. Bonds & surety (FL 255.05 — "Little Miller Act")

- **P&P bonds at 100% of contract value** are required on public work. There are **no
  lien rights against public property** — the payment bond is the subs'/suppliers' only
  remedy, so owners enforce this strictly. Bond must be recorded before starting work.
- **Thresholds (FL 255.05):** state contracts **≤ $100,000** — the agency **may
  exempt** the bond per DMS rules (not automatic). **Local governments** (counties,
  cities, districts) **may waive** the bond at their discretion on contracts
  **≤ $200,000**. DMS may delegate authority to state agencies
  to exempt contracts **> $100,000 but < $200,000**. Assume bonds required unless the
  solicitation says otherwise.
- **Bid bond:** typically **5% of the bid amount** (per bid form — a norm, not a
  statute; some owners require 10% or a fixed sum). Cashier's check sometimes accepted.
  Forfeited if you're awarded and fail to execute the contract/bonds.
- **Surety qualification:** owners commonly require the surety be admitted in FL and
  Treasury-listed (Circular 570) with underwriting limit ≥ contract value — check the
  supplementary conditions. Your **bonding capacity** (single/aggregate) is a real
  constraint: confirm capacity with your agent **before** chasing the job.
- Bond premium ~1–3% sliding scale (see methodology §5); it is a cost of work, applied
  once, on top of everything else it covers per your surety's rate filing.

## 4. Labor: no state prevailing wage; Davis-Bacon only if federal money

- **Florida has NO state prevailing wage law** (repealed 1979). State/county/city-funded
  work: bid open-shop market wages.
- **Local wage mandates are preempted:** FL **HB 433 (2024)** preempts local government
  regulation of wages/benefits for contractors; full preemption of local wage mandates
  takes effect **September 30, 2026**. Legacy ordinances (Miami-Dade, Orlando, etc.)
  may still appear in older solicitations in the transition window — **verify with
  counsel/AHJ** whether a local wage requirement in the bid docs is still enforceable,
  and price what the contract requires either way.
- **Davis-Bacon (federal)** applies when **federal funds are in the project** — contracts
  > **$2,000** for construction/alteration/repair (direct federal or federally assisted:
  FDOT federal-aid, HUD, FEMA, ARPA/IIJA grants, USDA, FAA). **How to tell:** look for
  the **wage determination (WD)** in the specs, FHWA/HUD/FEMA supplementary conditions,
  Buy America(n) clauses, or Form of Contract references to 29 CFR.
- **What Davis-Bacon does to labor pricing:**
  - Pay the **WD rate = base + fringe** per classification. If you don't provide bona
    fide fringes, the fringe is paid **in cash on top of base** — cash-paid fringe is
    excludable from the OT premium base per 29 CFR 5.32 (OT premium runs on the WD
    basic hourly rate), but it generally DOES count as payroll for workers'-comp/burden
    basis unless paid into bona fide benefit plans — so paying fringe in cash raises
    burden even though it does not raise the OT premium. Re-run the labor build-up
    (methodology §2) per classification; do not use market composite rates.
  - **Certified payroll (WH-347) weekly** with signed Statement of Compliance, for you
    and every sub — real admin cost: carry hours in Div 01 (payroll clerk / compliance
    software) and expect subs to add 2–5% for compliance on small trades.
  - Misclassification and missing payrolls hold up **all** payment. Flow the requirement
    down in every subcontract.
- **Buy America / BABA cost impact:** federal-aid work restricts steel, iron, and
  (post-IIJA) manufactured products/construction materials to domestic sources with
  melt-and-pour certs. Price the domestic product — expect premiums and longer leads
  on structural steel, pipe, gear, and rebar — and carry Div 01 hours for certification
  tracking. Confirm which regime applies (FHWA 23 CFR 635.410 vs. BABA 2 CFR 184) and
  whether any waivers are published for this program.

## 5. Owner Direct Purchase (ODP) — the FL sales-tax carve-out

Public owners hold a Consumer's Certificate of Exemption; contractors on real-property
contracts do not (contractor is the taxed consumer — see `florida-code.md` §5). ODP
programs (Rule **12A-1.094, F.A.C.**; FL 212.08(6)) let the owner buy major materials
directly and skip the sales tax:

- **Mechanics:** owner issues the **PO directly to the vendor** with its Certificate of
  Exemption and Certificate of Entitlement; **vendor invoices the owner; owner pays the
  vendor directly**; **owner takes title and risk of loss** from delivery until
  incorporation into the work. Contractor typically identifies candidate materials,
  prepares requisitions, receives and manages the material.
- **Estimate impact:** bid the job **with tax**, then carry a **deductive ODP allowance/
  alternate** per the bid form (owner deducts material cost + tax via change order and
  buys direct). Tax the *remaining* (non-ODP) materials only. Never simply zero-out tax
  in your base bid unless the bid form explicitly instructs it.
- **What qualifies:** big-ticket, discrete, deliverable materials — gear, AHUs/chillers,
  generators, structural steel packages, pipe. Not labor, not equipment rental, not
  small consumables (admin cost exceeds the tax saved below ~$10–20k per PO — set a
  floor with the owner).
- **Cash flow:** ODP dollars leave your contract value — your billings, bond premium
  basis, and fee basis shrink. Confirm whether **OH&P is preserved on ODP amounts**
  (most programs deduct cost + tax but leave your markup; some don't — read the ODP
  procedure before bidding).
- **Admin burden:** PO logs, requisition packages, delivery reconciliation, owner-titled
  material stored on your site (insurance/builder's risk must cover owner-owned stored
  materials — coordinate). Carry hours in Div 01.
- **Risk:** each owner PO must carry the owner's Certificate of Entitlement. When it
  does, Rule 12A-1.094(4) puts liability for tax, penalty, and interest **on the
  governmental entity** if the exemption fails — and the entity cannot shift that
  liability back to you by contract. You remain the taxed consumer only for purchases
  made outside the ODP mechanics (your own POs) and for materials you manufacture/
  fabricate yourself. Follow the rule's mechanics exactly so purchases stay inside the
  protected structure.

## 6. Retainage & prompt payment

- **Retainage capped at 5%** on FL public work: local governments per **FL 218.735**
  (Local Government Prompt Payment Act) and state entities per **FL 255.078** — flat
  5% cap for the whole job since **HB 101 (2020), effective October 1, 2020**
  (previously 10% dropping to 5% at 50% completion); **SB 346 (2023)** further
  tightened punch-list and prompt-pay/dispute timelines effective July 1, 2023. Owners
  may hold less, step down, or release early.
- Flow-down: you may withhold from subs at the same or lower rate — mirror the cap in
  subcontracts.
- **Prompt-payment chain (FL 218.70–218.80 local; 255.071–255.078 state):** statutory
  clocks on owner payment after a proper pay request, then on GC payment to subs after
  receipt; punch list creation deadlines; after punch list the owner pays remaining
  balance less **150% of the estimated cost** of punch items. Interest accrues on late
  payment. Know the clocks — they are your leverage; exact day-counts vary by entity
  type (verify per statute for the specific owner).

## 7. Award, protests, and public records

- **Responsiveness** = the bid conforms to the solicitation (all forms, bond, addenda
  acknowledged, no conditions). **Responsibility** = the bidder is capable (licenses,
  capacity, experience, past performance). Award goes to the **lowest responsive,
  responsible bidder**. Responsiveness defects are usually fatal and non-waivable except
  "minor irregularities" — never rely on the owner waiving one.
- **Bid protests — state agencies (FL 120.57(3)):** notice of protest within **72 hours**
  of posting of the decision/intended decision; formal written protest within **10 days**
  after; **protest bond** required (dismissal if not posted). Deadlines are jurisdictional.
- **Bid protests — local:** governed by the **local ordinance/purchasing code**, not Ch.
  120 (unless the entity adopted it). Timelines can be as short as 48–72 hours — pull
  the ordinance the day results post.
- **Public records (FL Ch. 119):** your bid becomes a public record. Sealed bids are
  temporarily exempt (FL 119.071(1)(b)) **until notice of an intended decision or 30
  days after opening, whichever is earlier** — after that, competitors can (and do)
  request your bid, unit prices, and sub list. Mark trade secrets per statute if you
  claim exemption (narrow; verify with counsel). Assume everything you submit is public.

## 8. Bid mechanics (responsiveness traps)

- **Mandatory pre-bid meeting/site visit:** if marked mandatory, missing it =
  non-responsive. Sign the sheet; keep proof.
- **Addenda:** acknowledge **every** addendum, by number, on the bid form. A missed
  acknowledgment of a material addendum = non-responsive. Check the portal the morning
  of bid day.
- **Cone of Silence / no-contact rules:** many FL local governments (Miami-Dade,
  Broward, Palm Beach, others) prohibit any communication about the solicitation with
  owner staff, evaluators, or elected officials except through the named procurement
  contact, from advertisement until award. Violation = disqualification. Route every
  question through the official RFI/portal channel and log it.
- **Sealed bid form:** ink signatures/corporate seal where required, all blanks filled
  (write "N/A", never leave blank), correct envelope labeling, delivered before the
  clock — late is late by seconds.
- **Unit-price schedules:** price every line; unbalanced bidding (overpricing early/
  certain-overrun items) can be grounds for rejection — keep it defensible.
- **Trench safety (FS 553.60–.64):** if the work includes trench excavation > 5 ft
  deep, the bid must reference the OSHA/trench-safety standards to be used and state
  the **cost of compliance as a separate line item** on the bid form — omitting it is
  treated as non-responsive by most owners. Carry the cost (sloping/benching, boxes,
  shoring) in Div 31, but also show it where the bid form asks.
- **No post-bid negotiation:** the number you open with is the number. No scope
  clarifications after opening; math errors resolved per the bid form's precedence rules
  (usually unit price governs over extension).
- **Bid withdrawal:** before opening — usually allowed by written request. After
  opening — only for demonstrable, documented clerical error, at the owner's discretion,
  typically claimed within a short window (verify the bid documents/ordinance); otherwise
  the bid bond is at risk.
- **Liquidated damages:** the norm on public work, set per day in the supplementary
  conditions; scale from hundreds/day (small municipal) to $10k+/day (transportation/
  critical facilities). Price schedule risk accordingly; LDs are rarely negotiable.
- **Front-loading the SOV:** public owners audit SOVs against the estimate and will
  reject or rebalance front-loaded values. Load Div 01/mobilization defensibly.
- **DBE/SBE/MBE goals:** meet the stated participation % or document **good-faith
  efforts** (solicitation logs, plan-room notices, follow-ups) on the owner's forms.
  Failure on either = non-responsive on many owners. Certified-firm quotes may run
  higher — price the goal, not the cheapest quote.

## 9. Sector-specific division emphasis

| Div | Change | Commonly missed |
|-----|--------|-----------------|
| 00 | **Grows** | Bid bond, P&P bond premium, DBE forms, public-entity crimes affidavit, e-Verify, Scrutinized Companies certs |
| 01 | **Grows** | Certified-payroll clerk (federal), ODP administration, owner's testing vs. GC testing split, progress photo/reporting requirements, project sign, on-site owner rep facilities |
| 02 | Watch | Public renovation work often has unsurveyed asbestos/lead — demand the survey or exclude via RFI (can't qualify the bid) |
| 03/05 | Watch | Prescriptive specs (no or-equal) force named suppliers — get quotes on the specified product |
| 03/05/07/08/26 | **Grow (schools)** | SREF / FBC Ch. 453 requirements on educational facilities; EHPA shelter hardening (impact assemblies, hardened envelope, standby power) — do not benchmark $/SF against non-school commercial |
| 26 | Note | Electrical-only local jobs bid competitively above the **FL 255.20** electrical trigger ($75k base, ENR-BCI-adjusted upward since 2009) — small electrical work is still formally bid |
| 31/32/33 | **Grow** | Heavy unit-price content (earthwork, pipe, paving); owner-estimated quantities; MOT/traffic control per FDOT indexes on road work; trench safety separate line item |
| All | — | ODP-eligible materials must be flagged per division at estimate time so the deduct can be assembled fast after award |

## 10. Markup & commercial posture

| Item | Public-sector norm |
|------|-------------------|
| **Bid bond** | ~5% of bid (per bid form) — no direct cost if surety line is open |
| **P&P bonds** | 100% of contract value (FL 255.05); premium ~1–3% sliding scale |
| **Retainage** | **5% max** (FL 218.735 / 255.078); mirror in subcontracts |
| **General Conditions** | Normal-to-high end of 8–15% — reporting, compliance, ODP admin, longer closeout |
| **OH&P** | **Tight**: commonly ~4–8% combined on hard-bid public work (public openings compress margin); do not import private-work 10–15% |
| **Insurance** | Per supplementary conditions — public entities often require higher GL limits, named additional insureds, waiver of subrogation; builder's risk must address ODP owner-titled stored materials |
| **Tax** | Materials taxed (contractor is consumer) EXCEPT ODP carve-out per Rule 12A-1.094 — structure per bid form |
| **Payment terms** | Statutory prompt-payment clocks (FL 218.70 et seq. / 255.071 et seq.); pay-when-paid flow-down; interest on late payment |
| **Escalation** | Carry it — award cycles run 60–120+ days (award, protest window, board approval, NTP); price validity on the bid form (often 90–120 days) must cover it |
| **Allowances / alternates** | Allowances rare; alternates priced exactly as designed, base bid clean — owners award on base + selected alternates in the order listed |

## 11. Sector red flags / deal-breakers

- [ ] Bonding capacity insufficient for this job + current backlog — **stop** before spending takeoff hours.
- [ ] Owner requires advance prequalification (FDOT Certificate of Qualification per FS 337.14 / Rule 14-22 for jobs > $250k; school-board or county annual prequal) and we are not currently prequalified or the window has closed — **stop** before takeoff.
- [ ] Mandatory pre-bid already occurred and we missed it — non-responsive; drop the pursuit.
- [ ] License mismatch: solicitation requires a license class/registration we don't hold (see DBPR classes in `florida-code.md` §5).
- [ ] Federal funds present but no WD in the docs — RFI immediately; a WD added by addendum late can blow up labor pricing.
- [ ] DBE goal higher than achievable sub coverage in this market and no good-faith-effort path.
- [ ] LDs disproportionate to job size, or schedule is unachievable as bid (no negotiation later).
- [ ] Unit-price quantities obviously wrong vs. takeoff and owner refuses to correct by addendum — over/under-run exposure.
- [ ] Owner's ODP procedure strips OH&P from deducted amounts (fee erosion).
- [ ] Bid validity period longer than our price protection from subs/suppliers — escalation gap.
- [ ] Protest-prone owner/low spread history — margin already thin; weigh pursuit cost.
- [ ] Local wage ordinance in docs during preemption transition — price it, and flag for counsel (HB 433; verify enforceability).

## 12. Deliverable additions (beyond the standard proposal package)

- **Executed bid form** — every blank, every addendum acknowledged by number, signatures/seal per instructions.
- **Bid bond** (or cashier's check if permitted) at the stated %.
- **DBE/SBE/MBE participation forms** + good-faith-effort documentation.
- **Public-entity crimes affidavit** (FL 287.133), **Scrutinized Companies** certification, **E-Verify** attestation, non-collusion affidavit — as included in the bid package.
- **Contractor license / qualifier documentation**, corporate authority resolution.
- **Experience/reference forms**, equipment lists, financials — if the owner pre-qualifies responsibility in the bid.
- **Unit-price schedule** fully priced; **ODP candidate-materials list** (if requested).
- Post-award: P&P bonds (recorded), insurance certificates per supplementary conditions, SOV (audit-ready), certified-payroll setup if federal.

## 13. Open items to confirm per job

1. Owner type (state agency vs. local) → which protest route (120.57(3) vs. ordinance), which prompt-pay statute, whose purchasing code.
2. Federal funds? → WD number/date in specs, Davis-Bacon + WH-347 flow-down, Buy America(n), any additional federal certs.
3. Bond requirements: waived below threshold? Surety qualification language? Bid bond % and form?
4. ODP program: exists? OH&P preserved on deducts? Minimum PO size? Who prepares requisitions?
5. Current ENR-BCI-adjusted 255.20 thresholds if the job is near the trigger (affects whether it's formally bid at all).
6. Retainage: owner holding the full 5% or a step-down schedule?
7. Addenda count as of bid morning; portal checked; acknowledgment lines match.
8. Bid validity period vs. sub/supplier price protection; escalation carried for the gap.
9. LD rate, milestone dates, NTP timing assumptions.
10. DBE/SBE goals, certification body accepted (owner's own vs. state UCP), forms due at bid vs. post-bid.
11. Local preference or local-vendor scoring in play (and whether restricted by funding source — verify with counsel/AHJ).
12. Any surviving local wage mandate in the docs (HB 433 transition — verify enforceability with counsel).
13. Public-records handling: what in our bid could we claim as trade secret, and is it worth it (verify with counsel).
14. Trench excavation > 5 ft in scope? Trench safety line item completed (FS 553.63)?
15. Prequalification current with this owner? Capacity rating covers this contract amount?
