# Commercial Buildout / Tenant Improvement (Florida)

Sector guide for interior buildouts of leased commercial space — office, retail,
restaurant, medical — inside an existing, usually occupied, landlord-controlled building.
**The building already exists, so existing conditions — not quantities — are the dominant
risk**, and a third party (the landlord) sits between you and the owner of record.
Baseline rules live in `florida-code.md`, `estimating-methodology.md`, and
`csi-divisions.md`; this doc covers only what TI changes.

---

## 1. What changes vs. the default pipeline

| Stage | TI-specific change |
|-------|--------------------|
| **Takeoff** | Quantities come from a **field-verification walk**, not just drawings — as-builts lie. Add above-ceiling survey, panel schedules, RTU nameplates, demising wall ratings. Demo takeoff is as important as new-work takeoff. |
| **Procurement** | **Landlord-mandated vendors** (fire alarm, sprinkler, roofing, BAS — see §5) cannot be competitively bid — carry their quotes or a fat allowance. Pre-purchase long-lead items against an 8–14 week schedule. |
| **Scope** | Add lease-driven scope: work letter, building standards, restoration clauses, landlord approval cycles. Classify every demo item stay/go/salvage. Assign tie-in scope (drain-down, fire watch, shutdown coordination) to a named party. |
| **Estimate** | Contingency **10–15%** (existing conditions), not the new-construction norm. Div 01 carries after-hours premiums, protection, escorts. Price mandated-vendor quotes as-is, no shopping. |
| **Proposal** | Qualifications must state field-verification limits, hazmat exclusions/allowances, landlord fees carried or excluded, after-hours assumptions, and what the TI allowance does/doesn't cover. |
| **Audit** | Check: hazmat survey addressed, path-of-travel (ADA) carried, mandated vendors priced, fire-watch/impairment costs present, contingency ≥10%, restoration clause addressed, $/SF within the TI band for the use type. |

## 2. Existing conditions — the core risk

**Never price a TI from the drawings alone.** Mandatory pre-bid verification walk:

- **Above-ceiling survey** — lift tiles in a grid; map ductwork, piping, conduit,
  cabling, structure, plenum rating. Congestion above the ceiling is the #1 unpriced cost.
- **Electrical** — photograph **panel schedules**; verify spare breaker capacity and
  physical space vs. new load. No spares = new panel/feeder = possible service upgrade
  (big dollars, long lead on gear). Verify the meter/house-power boundary with the LL.
- **HVAC** — record **RTU/AHU age, tonnage, refrigerant** from nameplates. R-22 units
  are end-of-life; verify who owns replacement (lease). Verify actual tonnage vs. the
  new occupant load — designers routinely assume capacity that isn't there.
- **Structure** — verify capacity **before adding loads** (rooftop units, mezzanines,
  compact filing, dental chairs, med equipment). Engage the LL's structural EOR; carry a fee.
- **Slab** — **GPR/x-ray before any coring or saw-cut** (PT tendons, conduits in slab,
  underground utilities under SOG). Under-slab plumbing for restaurants/medical means
  trench-and-patch — walk the route, price the patch-back.
- **Demising walls & ratings** — confirm what's actually rated and where walls really
  stop (deck vs. ceiling). Fire-caulk gaps found above ceilings are your problem once you touch them.
- Document everything with photos; attach the survey to the bid as a basis-of-estimate exhibit.

## 3. Asbestos / lead (Div 02)

- **Florida requires a written asbestos survey before renovation or demolition of a
  commercial building**, performed by a **licensed asbestos consultant** (mandate: **FDEP Rule 62-257, F.A.C. /
  NESHAP**, administered locally — e.g. Pinellas County Air Quality; **FL Ch. 469**
  licenses who may perform it; survey report must be on site during renovation).
- **Federal NESHAP (40 CFR 61 Subpart M):** notification to DEP/local program
  **10 working days** before demolition (required even if no asbestos) or before
  renovation disturbing RACM ≥ **260 LF pipe / 160 SF other components / 35 CF**.
- **Pre-1981 buildings:** OSHA **29 CFR 1926.1101** treats thermal system insulation and
  surfacing material as **presumed ACM (PACM)** until sampled — heightened risk on any
  older strip center or office tower.
- **Who pays abatement:** normally the **landlord/owner** (it's their building), but the
  lease/work letter governs — confirm in writing. In the bid: **exclude abatement, carry
  the survey**, or carry an owner-directed allowance. Never silently include it.
- Lead paint: pre-1978 finishes — OSHA worker protection applies (federal); RRP rule is
  residential/child-occupied only. Note and exclude testing/abatement unless directed.
- **Mold:** pre-existing mold behind ceilings/walls is a routine FL discovery; assessment
  and remediation require **separately licensed firms** (FS 468 Part XVI — mold assessor/
  remediator). Exclude remediation, define a stop-work/notify protocol in the
  qualifications, and note that LL/owner normally pays (lease governs — confirm in
  writing, same as abatement).

## 4. Code triggers — FBC-Existing Building & ADA

- TI work is governed by **FBC-Existing Building (8th Ed., 2023; 9th Ed. effective
  Dec 31, 2026 for later permits)**. Classification of work:
  **Level 1** (Ch. 7 — finishes/equipment replacement, no space reconfiguration),
  **Level 2** (Ch. 8 — reconfiguration of space, doors/windows, systems work), **Level 3**
  (Ch. 9 — work area exceeds **50% of building area**; pulls in building-wide upgrades).
  Most TIs are Level 2. Confirm the classification on the code summary — it drives
  sprinkler, alarm, and egress upgrade scope.
- **Change of occupancy** (Ch. 10): triggered when the new use demands more structural,
  fire, egress, accessibility, or ventilation capacity than exists (e.g., office → assembly
  or medical). Can trigger building-wide upgrades far beyond the suite — flag early, price the delta.
- **Flood 50% rule:** repair/alteration cost ≥ **50% of the structure's market value** =
  **substantial improvement** → full flood compliance (FBC-EB flood provisions). Mostly
  bites ground-floor TIs in AE/VE zones — see `florida-code.md` §4.
- **ADA path of travel (28 CFR 36.403 — federal):** altering a **primary function area**
  obligates upgrades to the path of travel, restrooms, phones, and drinking fountains
  serving it, **up to 20% of the cost of the primary alteration** (disproportionality cap),
  with a **3-year lookback** aggregating prior alterations. Carry restroom/route upgrades
  or a stated allowance; confirm LL vs. tenant responsibility (common-area restrooms are
  usually LL's). FBC-Accessibility applies in parallel.
- **Energy code:** replaced HVAC units, lighting, and altered envelope must meet current
  **FBC-Energy** — a reused-vs-replaced decision changes code scope, not just equipment cost.
- **Electrical:** altered/extended circuits generally must meet current NEC as adopted by
  the FBC — **GFCI expansion bites commercial TIs** (NEC 210.8(B) — receptacles near sinks,
  kitchens, rooftops, indoor damp locations; 406.4(D) on replaced receptacles); AFCI is
  generally N/A outside dwelling/dormitory/guest-room occupancies — relevant only for
  residential-type TI (e.g., hotel suites). Verify extent with AHJ.
- **Restaurant/food service:** DBPR Division of Hotels & Restaurants **plan review +
  license required before opening** (FDACS for retail food); grease-interceptor sizing
  subject to the sewer utility's FOG program — separate submittals, fees, and 2–6+ week
  review time on top of the building permit. Confirm who submits (usually the kitchen
  consultant) and build it into the schedule.

## 5. The landlord ecosystem

- **Work letter types:** **turnkey** (LL builds to spec — you may be bidding to the LL),
  **TI allowance** ($/SF credit, tenant contracts the work — most common), **as-is**
  (tenant carries everything). Read the work letter before pricing — it *is* the scope.
- **What burns the TI allowance** (confirm each): design/engineering fees, permit fees,
  **LL construction supervision/oversight fee (commonly 1–5% of cost — per lease, verify)**,
  mandated-vendor costs, sometimes FF&E/cabling (often excluded). Tenant pays overage —
  your estimate vs. allowance gap is the client's real decision number.
- **Building standards:** LL's base-building spec (ceiling tile, light fixtures, door/
  hardware, HVAC boxes, blinds) — **match it or get written variance**. Price the standard
  product, not the cheapest equal.
- **Landlord-mandated vendors — you cannot bid these:** fire alarm, sprinkler, roofing
  (warranty protection), BAS/controls, riser management (any cabling in building risers/
  telecom rooms), sometimes MEP and locksmith. Their pricing runs
  high; get quotes early, carry them as named subs or allowances, and qualify that they
  are non-competitive. Never plug market unit prices for mandated trades.
- **LL plan approval:** LL review cycle (often 2–4 weeks, sometimes with a review fee)
  runs **before or parallel to permitting** — build it into the schedule.
- **Restoration/removal clauses:** lease may require demo of specialty improvements at
  lease end (internal stairs, supplemental HVAC, vaults). Not usually in your bid, but
  flag it — it changes what the tenant should build.
- **Insurance/COI:** LL dictates limits, additional insureds, sometimes umbrella. Get the
  lease exhibit before finalizing insurance cost.

## 6. Occupied-building logistics (Div 01 — the TI premium)

- **After-hours work** for demo, coring, odor (paint/adhesives/welding), noise near
  occupied suites — LL rules dictate; assume nights/weekends for demo unless told otherwise.
  Apply overtime premium + productivity loss.
- **Freight elevator** scheduling (reservations, padding, operator fees), **loading dock
  windows**, material staging limits (often none — daily hand-carry), **parking** for crews (paid?).
- **Protection of common areas:** lobby/corridor floor protection, elevator cab protection,
  temporary partitions at suite entry — LL will back-charge damage.
- **Security escorts / badging** for after-hours and roof access — some towers charge for the escort.
- **No-hot-work windows / hot-work permits** with fire watch per LL and FL Fire Prevention Code.
- **Negative air / dust control** at demising walls and corridor doors near occupied
  suites; odor control on finishes.
- **ICRA (Infection Control Risk Assessment)** if the building is healthcare-occupied —
  anterooms, HEPA neg-air, sealed carts, monitoring. Substantial Div 01 cost; also AHCA
  requirements if the *project* is a licensed healthcare facility (verify with AHJ/AHCA).

## 7. MEP tie-ins to base building

| Tie-in | Cost/scope items commonly missed |
|--------|----------------------------------|
| **Sprinkler (21)** | Drain-down/refill of the zone, **impairment permit/notice to FD & LL**, **fire watch** during impairment, after-hours tie-in, mandated vendor |
| **Fire alarm (28)** | LL vendor tie-in fee, new devices programmed into base panel, **after-hours acceptance testing** with FD, monitoring company coordination |
| **HVAC (23)** | **Condenser-water or chilled-water taps** (tap fees, drain-down, LL vendor), supplemental cooling 24/7 loads (server rooms), TAB of affected zones |
| **BAS (25)** | **Integration fee to LL controls vendor** — points, graphics, licensing; non-negotiable pricing |
| **Electrical (26)** | Building **shutdown coordination** (after-hours, LL notice period), house-meter vs. tenant-meter, new meter/CT can fee to utility |

## 8. HVAC reuse-vs-replace framework

- Reuse if: unit < ~10–12 yrs, non-R-22, tonnage matches new load, LL accepts condition.
- Replace if: R-22, > 15 yrs, undersized, or lease makes tenant own it going forward —
  replacement triggers **current FBC-Energy efficiency** and possibly curb adapters,
  structural check, crane day, and HVHZ tie-down/NOA if exposed (see `florida-code.md` §2).
- Either way price: duct cleaning/reuse assessment, new distribution to layout, controls,
  TAB, and a **pre-lease-execution mechanical condition report** (recommend one; carry the fee).

## 9. Demo scope discipline (Div 02)

- Written stay/go matrix: **demising walls** (rated — usually stay), **ceiling grid**
  (reuse grid + new tile is common — confirm condition), doors/frames/hardware (salvage
  for reuse?), flooring (demo + prep: **floor prep/leveling is the classic miss** — carry SF of patch/skim).
- **Salvage credit** only with a buyer in hand; otherwise it's disposal cost.
- Waste streams: separate hazmat, universal waste (**lamps/ballasts — mercury/PCB,
  required recycling/handling**), metals (rebate), general C&D. Dumpster location per LL —
  sometimes none available → haul-off pricing.
- Cap/safe-off MEP at demo: who traces and safes circuits? Assign it (02 vs. 26).

## 10. Schedule compression & long-lead

- Rent commencement / free-rent burn-off drives everything; typical TI build =
  **8–14 weeks**. Compare long-lead deliveries against that:
  doors/frames/**hardware** (8–16 wks), storefront/**glass**, **switchgear/panels**
  (worst offender — can exceed the whole schedule), **RTUs**, custom **millwork**,
  specialty lighting.
- **Pre-purchase strategy:** release long-lead with a letter of intent / early-release PO
  before permit; qualify storage, deposits, and cancellation exposure in the proposal.
- **Permit expediting:** private provider plan review/inspections (FL 553.791) and local
  expediters are legitimate schedule buys — price as a Div 01 line, not overhead.
- Phased/early-start permits (demo permit first) where the AHJ allows.

## 12. Estimating posture & $/SF bands

Reasonableness bands only — confirm with current quotes (see `estimating-methodology.md` preamble):

| TI type | $/SF band (2025–26 FL, shell to keys) | Premium drivers |
|---------|----------------------------------------|-----------------|
| Vanilla / spec office | ~$60–120 | Ceiling reuse, building-standard finishes |
| Full office buildout | ~$120–200+ | Glass fronts, pantry plumbing, supplemental HVAC, AV |
| Retail (white box → full) | ~$50–200 | Storefront, brand-standard finishes |
| Restaurant | ~$250–500+ | **Kitchen equipment, grease duct/shaft, Ansul, makeup air, grease interceptor, gas** |
| Medical/dental | ~$200–400+ | **Plumbing density, med-gas, lead-lined walls (imaging), HVAC filtration, ICRA** |

- **Contingency: 10–15%** on existing-conditions risk (vs. lower new-construction norms).
  Reduce only with a documented verification walk and above-ceiling survey.
- **GC%/Div 01:** compressed schedule ≠ cheap GCs — short duration but heavy supervision,
  after-hours, and logistics; build from the actual staffing plan, often **10–15%** of direct.
- Carry **LL pass-throughs** as separate lines: supervision fee, review fees, elevator/
  dock charges, badging, utilities during construction (per lease — who pays temp power?).

## 13. Markup & commercial posture

| Item | TI norm |
|------|---------|
| **Bond** | Rarely required by tenants; **LL may require P&P bond or LOC** on large TIs (lease exhibit). Public tenant (gov lease) → FL 255.05 **may** apply — fact-specific (whether the TI is a "public building"/"public work," e.g., publicly funded improvements); confirm bonding requirement in the government lease/solicitation before pricing. ~1–3% if required. |
| **Retainage** | Private: **no FL statutory cap — per contract** (commonly 10%, reduce to 5% at 50%; negotiate). Public work: **5% cap** (FS 218.735 local / FS 255.078 state). |
| **GC/OH&P** | Combined OH&P **8–15%** — small, fast jobs sit at the high end; negotiated/CM work often fee + GCs open-book. |
| **Insurance** | GL per LL lease exhibit (limits + additional insureds: LL, property manager, lender). Builder's risk often via LL's property policy — **confirm, don't double-carry**. |
| **Tax** | Real-property improvement: contractor pays FL sales tax on materials (6% + surtax) — see `florida-code.md` §5. County surtax applies only to the **first $5,000 per single item** (FS 212.054(2)(b)) — flat combined rates over-tax big-ticket units (RTUs, switchgear). Watch trade-fixture/FF&E scope (can be taxed as retail sale — confirm contract structure). |
| **Payment** | Private: FL Ch. 713 lien law fully applies — **NTO to owner AND lessee/LL as applicable**. Check public records for a recorded **FS 713.10 notice** — if the lease prohibits liens on the fee, **your lien attaches to the leasehold only** (weak security on tenant default). Evaluate tenant credit; on thin-credit tenants require allowance escrow/joint checks, LL joinder, deposit, or personal guaranty. Pay apps often routed through LL's TI-allowance disbursement (draw conditions, lien releases per draw). |
| **Wage rules** | Davis-Bacon (**federal**) only if federally funded/leased; no FL prevailing wage on private TI. |

## 14. Sector red flags / deal-breakers

- [ ] No asbestos survey provided on a pre-1981 building — **stop; survey before demo bid** (FL Ch. 469 / NESHAP).
- [ ] Bid due before a verification walk is possible — qualify heavily or decline.
- [ ] Panel schedules missing/full and drawings add load — service upgrade lurking.
- [ ] Change of occupancy (office→assembly/medical) not addressed on code summary.
- [ ] Ground-floor TI in AE/VE flood zone approaching the 50% substantial-improvement threshold.
- [ ] Mandated-vendor list not provided — you're bidding trades you can't buy.
- [ ] Work letter/lease exhibits not in the bid package — scope boundary unknown.
- [ ] Restaurant TI with no grease interceptor shown — exterior site work hiding in a "TI."
- [ ] Restoration clause requires removal of what you're building — advise client.
- [ ] Schedule assumes permit + LL approval overlap that the LL doesn't allow.
- [ ] R-22 or 15+ yr RTUs shown "reuse existing" with no condition report.
- [ ] Occupied healthcare adjacency with no ICRA scope or budget.
- [ ] Recorded FS 713.10 lien prohibition + thin-credit tenant + allowance paid in arrears —
  your lien is leasehold-only; secure payment another way or decline.
- [ ] Older building / multiple prior tenants with no as-built history — unpermitted legacy
  work discovered at demo becomes your permit problem; qualify that correction of
  pre-existing code violations and unpermitted prior work is excluded / handled by change order.

## 15. Deliverable additions (beyond the standard bid package)

- **Existing-conditions survey report** (photos, panel schedules, RTU nameplates,
  above-ceiling notes) as a basis-of-estimate exhibit.
- **Assumptions & exclusions rider** specific to TI: hazmat, correction of pre-existing
  violations & unpermitted prior work, LL fees, mandated-vendor pricing basis,
  after-hours assumptions, floor-prep allowance basis.
- **TI allowance reconciliation** — estimate vs. allowance, showing tenant overage.
- **Long-lead procurement log** with release-by dates vs. the schedule.
- **Logistics plan** (elevator/dock/staging/after-hours) for LL approval.
- **Impairment/shutdown matrix** — each tie-in, notice period, fire-watch duration, who pays.
- **Allowance/alternate schedule** per `estimating-methodology.md` §6.

## 16. Open items to confirm per job

1. AHJ + FBC edition on code summary; alteration Level (1/2/3) and any change of occupancy.
2. Asbestos survey status; who pays abatement if found (lease).
3. Work letter type (turnkey / allowance / as-is); allowance $/SF and what burns it.
4. LL-mandated vendor list + their quotes; LL supervision fee %.
5. Building standards document; deviations requiring LL variance.
6. Panel capacity, RTU condition report, structural sign-off for added loads.
7. After-hours rules, elevator/dock booking, protection requirements, escort/badging costs.
8. ADA path-of-travel scope split (LL common areas vs. tenant suite) and 20% cap math (28 CFR 36.403).
9. Flood zone + substantial-improvement math on ground-floor coastal suites.
10. Insurance exhibit (limits, additional insureds, builder's risk carrier), retainage terms, draw/lien-release procedure through the LL.
11. Permit path: standard vs. private provider (FL 553.791) vs. expediter; demo-first permit availability.
12. Restoration/removal obligations at lease end (advise, even if excluded).
13. On restaurant TIs: DBPR H&R plan-review status and FOG/interceptor sizing approval —
    who submits, fee, timeline.
